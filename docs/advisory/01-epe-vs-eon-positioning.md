## Risk framing for the client

Switching an active high-volume framework mid-programme to a DNO-affiliated ICP carries three concrete risks:

1. **Conflict of interest on POC allocation.** A DNO-owned ICP has no commercial incentive to challenge its own group's POC quotation, reinforcement scope, or connection charge. EPE routinely negotiates these down on client's behalf.
2. **Re-mobilisation lag.** Any change of ICP re-triggers site surveys, design reviews, permit re-application and TM plan re-approval. Realistic slippage: 8–14 weeks per site in flight.
3. **Split accountability.** When the ICP is the DNO group, the client loses independent escalation. Every dispute becomes an internal utility conversation the client isn't in.
